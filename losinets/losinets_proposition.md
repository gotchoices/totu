# Losinets collaboration — proposition

**Purpose.** How to approach Dmitry Losinets with the cross-framework findings assembled in `losinets/losinets_summary.md`. What to lead with, what to ask, in what form, and what to expect back.

**Status.** Draft; no contact has been made. All substantive results referenced here are complete (R60–R63 plus the α↔R derivation).

**Prerequisites.** Read `losinets/losinets_summary.md` first. This document assumes those findings are understood and focuses entirely on the communication / collaboration question.

---

## 1. What Losinets is likely to value

Read in his own voice across the three papers, Losinets values:

- **Exact derivation over analogy.** He distinguishes formal isomorphism from ontological claim and is careful not to overclaim. "This paper is purely formal: we establish a mathematical isomorphism… No ontological claim is made that electromagnetism is mechanical."
- **Falsifiable predictions with specific numbers and experimental proposals.** Every paper ends with numbered experimental tests.
- **Clear scope and explicitly flagged open problems.** m_vac open in photon §2.4, spin-½ open in baryon §2.2, G_shape lower bound in EfD §2.1. He labels what he hasn't derived.
- **Mechanical / pedagogical intuition.** The baryon paper explicitly notes educational applications as a goal.
- **Modesty about scope.** "Phenomenological," "not fundamental," "serves as a convenient tool."

**What will turn him off:**

- A correspondence table presented as a theorem.
- Claims that extend his framework without mechanism.
- Pitches that lead with MaSt's T⁶ machinery, the 9×9 metric, or the three-generation shear construction — that is MaSt's interior and reads as unmotivated to someone coming in through viscoelastic continuum mechanics.
- Overselling the α^(−1/3) ≈ 5.2 coincidence as a derivation.
- Assuming he'll adopt MaSt's framework wholesale.

## 2. What we can actually offer

Distilled from R60–R63 + the α↔R derivation, in the order Losinets is most likely to care about:

### Offer 1: A Planck-scale cross-framework prediction — R ≈ t_P

The dimensionally correct Planck-scale estimate of his K-V relaxation time R = η₀/G, using G_shear = c⁴/(8πG_N) and η₀ ≈ ρ₀·c·L_P, gives

> R ≈ t_P ≈ 5.4 × 10⁻⁴⁴ s

If GRID's discrete 4D causal lattice is the long-wavelength microstructure of his K-V continuum, then his R is the Planck time. This is a **specific falsifiable number** at the substrate layer, testable in principle via superfluid-vortex analogues or careful PINEM studies that pin the K-V dissipation scale. His framework does not produce this number on its own; GRID's does, and the two frameworks have to agree if they describe the same substrate from different sides.

**Why this offer.** Substrate-layer prediction, his language (R, ν₀, G), our value (GRID's ζ = 1/4 axiom + its gravitational-constant derivation), zero speculation. It is also the kind of "concrete mechanical property measurable from the medium" that his EfD §4.6 section explicitly frames.

### Offer 2: A derived match for his mantle — 1.26 fm from 0.4 fm core

R61 shows that the exterior Hill-vortex stream function ψ = A·sin²θ/r — **his own formula** from EfD Appendix F — decays with angle-averaged energy density going as 1/r⁶. If the rotational core is supplied by MaSt's proton T⁶ eigenmode at ~0.4 fm (R60), then the "end-of-wake" scale at a 10⁻³ energy threshold is

> r_mantle = r_core · (10⁻³)^(−1/6) = 0.4 fm × 3.16 = 1.26 fm

— within 3% of Hofstadter's 1.3 fm mantle. The proton↔neutron core-reversal convention is preserved identically because |u|² is insensitive to the sign of Γ.

**Why this offer.** Uses his equations directly, cites his Appendix F, respects his core/mantle distinction (rotational vs irrotational-but-circulating), and gives him a quantitative result he could not derive from his own framework alone because the core scale comes from MaSt. The pitch is explicitly complementarity, not equivalence.

**Caveats to acknowledge up front:** The η = 10⁻³ threshold is an assumption-of-regime, not a derivation; two other nearby candidates (pion Compton 1.41 fm; α^(−1/3)·r_c = 1.29 fm) also land within 10%.

### Offer 3: A candidate mechanism for his baryon §2.2 open problem

MaSt's "odd tube-winding → spin-½" rule reproduces the spin parity for electron, pion, proton, neutron, and Σ⁻ (R63 inventory). Translated into K-V vocabulary, it becomes: **two Hopf-linked Hill rings with odd linking number close the joint phase only after two circuits of the primary**, so the effective Onsager–Feynman quantum per single circuit is Γ/2 and the angular-momentum functional is halved.

This is the M1 mechanism from R63. It matches his compound-rule table row-by-row. It does **not** yet have a K-V phase derivation — and that is exactly where we'd ask for his collaboration:

> Would you work out the phase integral along a Hopf-linked ring pair and see whether the closure condition drops out?

**Why this offer.** It addresses an open problem he himself flagged, in his own language, and it ends with a specific technical request he is uniquely equipped to answer. It also aligns with his stated interest in mechanical pictures of compound-particle structure.

### Offer 4: A negative result that clarifies m_vac

R62 shows MaSt's L-values span 25 orders of magnitude in implied m_vac across four identifications. The I-1/I-3 disagreement is 257×, decomposed exactly as (L₆/L₂)²·(m_p/m_e) = 257.

This means **MaSt does not fix m_vac.** For him that clears the table — it tells him MaSt is not an end-run around the photon §2.4 elastic-energy closure he flagged; his own programme still has to do that work. In the course of getting there we also noticed that §2.4 and §2.2 are structurally the same problem (both require the full K-V elastic functional, not just classical kinetic), which sharpens his own open-problem list.

**Why this offer.** Honest negative results signal we are not cherry-picking. It also converts "we don't know how to fix m_vac" into "you correctly identified where the work is."

### Offer 5 (speculative): a numerical coincidence worth explaining or dismissing

α^(−1/3) = 5.156 lands within 1% of his empirical r_m/r_c ≈ 5.2. Neither framework produces α as a 3-D geometric exponent. Flagged as either noise or a structural hint. Worth mentioning **as a curiosity, not as a result** — and explicitly leaving the interpretation open.

**Why this offer.** It invites him into a puzzle without claiming an answer. Intellectually honest; keeps the door open.

## 4. How to deliver the pitch

**Recommendation.** A single short note (2–3 pages) plus the completed summary document as appendix.

The short note should contain, in order:

- One paragraph: who we are, what MaSt and GRID are, why we read his three papers together.
- One paragraph: that we tried five cross-framework derivations and most failed — honest up front.
- Offers 1–4 as four compact paragraphs (substrate prediction, mantle match, spin-½ candidate, m_vac negative result).
- The four questions above.
- One closing line: happy to send the full working document, happy to adjust the framing, no expectation of agreement.

The appendix is the summary document.

**What to avoid in the short note:**

- MaSt's T⁶, 9×9 metric, three generations from shear resonance, neutrino mass predictions, nuclear scaling. None of this is needed to communicate the cross-framework findings. Keep it in reserve for if he engages.
- GRID axioms A1–A6 in detail. Cite the Planck-scale result; leave the axiomatic derivation for follow-up.
- The trilogy note or the earlier layer map. They were scaffolding; they are superseded by the summary.
- Any language that suggests we think his framework and ours describe the same physics.

**What to lead with in the first sentence.** "Your K-V mechanical analogy for Maxwell's equations admits a concrete Planck-scale prediction — R = t_P — when coupled to a discrete-lattice substrate, and your two-zone nucleon mantle at 1.3 fm drops out of your own Hill-vortex exterior (Appendix F) when the rotational core is supplied at ~0.4 fm."

That sentence tells him in 40 words: (a) we read his papers carefully, (b) we have a specific quantitative result, (c) we use his own equations, (d) we respect his framework's scope. Everything else follows.

## 5. What to expect back

Three plausible responses, in rough order of likelihood:

**Response A — polite dismissal.** "Thanks for the interest, this is outside my current research direction." Probability: moderate. Losinets's papers are careful and narrow; he may not want to expand scope. If this happens, ask whether he would flag any specific errors in the R61 wake calculation or the R62 m_vac analysis. A technical critique is itself useful.

**Response B — specific technical pushback.** "Your 10⁻³ threshold is arbitrary / the Hopf-linking idea needs more work / the R = t_P estimate uses the wrong G." Probability: moderate. This is the best outcome short of full engagement — it tells us which pieces he finds plausible and which need strengthening.

**Response C — engagement.** "I'd like to see the full summary / happy to think about the Hopf-ring phase integral / let's talk about §2.4." Probability: lower but non-trivial. If it happens, the right follow-up is a technical working session, not a paper draft. He will want to check the details himself before committing to anything joint.

**Non-responses we should treat as soft dismissal.** No reply after 2 weeks → one brief follow-up, then let it rest.
