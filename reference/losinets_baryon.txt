                                                        Abstract
            This work develops a self-consistent extension of the Kelvin¡Voigt (K¡V) me-
        chanical analogy for Maxwell's equations derived in [6] to the description of the
        magnetic moments of baryons. Each nucleon is modelled as a single Hill-type vor-
        tex comprising a compact inner zone (core, radius rc) and an extended outer zone
        (mantle, outer radius rm), whose parameters are fixed by electron-scattering data
        on the charge distribution. Within the K¡V framework, the electric charge of a
        vortex ring is the mass-normalised circulation invariant Q = 0r0; the magnetic
        moment is derived from the peripheral velocity of the vortex core. The equal-charge
        condition yields a parameter-free relation between the magnetic moment ratio and
        the geometric size ratio of the two zones, in quantitative agreement with exper-
        iment. The model is extended to the hyperon sector: a falsifiable prediction is
        formulated for the  magnetic form factor, the  triplet is shown to require inde-
        pendent charge-distribution data, and the  doublet is found to be incompatible
        with the equal-charge ansatz under the K¡V sign convention; in contrast to the 
        case, where the failure is quantitative, the  system admits no physical solution at
        all. Concrete proposals for experimental verification are developed. The approach
        is phenomenological and does not claim to be fundamental, but possesses significant
        potential for educational applications.

1 Introduction

Experimental studies of electron scattering on nucleons [1, 2], including the pioneering
work of Hofstadter and the review by Schopper [3], have revealed a complex internal
structure of the proton and neutron, indicating the presence of a compact core ("kernel")
and an extended envelope ("mantle") [4]. The standard interpretation of these data relies
on the quark model.

    In this work, we propose an alternative, phenomenological approach, building on the
Kelvin¡Voigt mechanical analogy for Maxwell's equations rigorously derived in [6]. In that
framework a dilute assembly of compact vortex rings in an incompressible K¡V viscoelastic
medium obeys equations formally identical to Maxwell's equations; the electric charge is
identified with the mass-normalised circulation invariant Q = 0r0. A key development
presented here is the self-consistent extension of this framework from the description of
macroscopic electromagnetic fields to the modelling of intrinsic particle properties--in
particular, the magnetic moments of nucleons. The aim of the work is to construct an
intuitively understandable and mathematically self-consistent model that can serve as a
bridge between classical and quantum concepts in the educational process.

6 Conclusion

The Kelvin¡Voigt mechanical analogy of [6] has been extended to a self-consistent de-
scription of nucleon magnetic moments. In the K¡V framework the electric charge is the
circulation invariant Q = 0r0 (eq. (1)), and the magnetic moment is Á = 0V (r0)2
(eq. (3)), with both quantities expressed through the same microscopic vortex parame-
ters. The equal-charge condition rcc = rmm yields the key prediction Ác/Ám = rm/rc
(eq. (11)). The predicted value 5.34 is consistent with the geometric ratio rm/rc = 5.2▒1.2
(eq. (12)); the comparison is limited by the uncertainty of the 1960s charge-distribution
data rather than by the precision of the magnetic moment measurements.

    The same geometric relation Ác/Ám = rm/rc has been extended to the hyperon sector.
For the  (isospin singlet), it provides a falsifiable prediction (eq. (14)) that connects the 
magnetic and charge form factors and can be tested at future electron¡hyperon scattering
facilities. For the  isospin triplet, the double-reversal scheme applied to +/- is found
to be inconsistent with experiment, indicating that the equal-charge ansatz does not
transfer directly from the nucleon sector, and that an independent measurement of the
 charge distribution is required. For the  doublet, the equal-charge ansatz applied to
the neutral member 0 forces opposite-sign zone circulations, leading to a system with

no physical solution for the zone magnetic moments--an inconsistency analogous to, and
more fundamental than, the  case. No charge-distribution data exist for the , making
a measurement of GE()(Q2) at future facilities the necessary prerequisite for any K¡V
prediction in the double-strangeness sector.

    The pattern of outcomes across the baryon octet--exact agreement at S = 0, underde-
termination at S = -1 (), quantitative failure at S = -1 (), and fundamental impos-
sibility at S = -2 ()--constitutes in itself a systematic research programme (Sec. 3.6,
Table 3): each inconsistency translates into a concrete structural prediction about the cor-
responding baryon, falsifiable through independent measurements of the electromagnetic
form factors at future hyperon scattering facilities.

    The constructed phenomenological two-zone model offers an intuitive picture of baryon
internal structure and significant advantages for educational applications. Explicit pro-
posals for experimental verification are formulated.
