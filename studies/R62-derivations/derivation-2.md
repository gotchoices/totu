# Derivation 2 — Kaluza-Klein on a 2-torus

**Program 1, Track 2.**  Generalize Kaluza–Klein from one compact
dimension (a circle) to two compact dimensions (a 2-torus).
Identify the U(1)×U(1) gauge structure that emerges, derive the
6D Christoffel symbols and geodesic equation, project to 4D,
and characterize the new ingredient — the **internal shear**
g_45 — that has no analog in 5D KK.

The test particle here is **massive** (timelike geodesic),
matching the primer's Appendix A.  This derivation does the
geometric/algebraic groundwork; the null-trajectory (photon)
case is treated separately.

---

## Inputs

In addition to derivation-1's inputs (SR, Lorentz invariance,
mostly-plus signature):

1. **Differential geometry of pseudo-Riemannian manifolds.**
   Metric tensor G_AB, inverse G^AB, Christoffel symbols
   Γ^A_BC = ½ G^AD (∂_B G_DC + ∂_C G_DB − ∂_D G_BC), geodesic
   equation d²x^A/dτ² + Γ^A_BC ẋ^B ẋ^C = 0.
2. **A massive test particle** following a 6D timelike geodesic,
   with G_AB ẋ^A ẋ^B = −c² and τ the proper time.
3. **The cylinder condition** (Klein 1926) extended to two
   compact directions: every component of G_AB depends only on
   the four spacetime coordinates x^μ, not on the two compact
   coordinates x^4 or x^5.  Justification: the compact
   directions are small enough that no probe to date has
   resolved variations along them, so fields are constant
   across them.
4. **Killing's theorem.** For each Killing vector ξ^A of the
   metric, the quantity ξ_A ẋ^A is conserved along any
   geodesic.

Signature throughout: (−, +, +, +, +, +) — spacetime is
mostly-plus, both compact directions are spacelike.  Indices:

| Range | Symbol | Meaning |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 0–3 | μ, ν, ρ, σ | spacetime |
| 4, 5 | a, b, c | compact (the 2-torus) |
| 0–5 | A, B, C, D | all six |

---

## Section A — Geometry and the metric ansatz

### A.1 — Coordinates and ranges

> *Purpose: nail down what the 6D manifold actually is.*

The manifold is M⁴ × T², a product of 4D Minkowski-like spacetime
with a 2-torus.  Coordinates:

- x⁰ ∈ ℝ — time
- x¹, x², x³ ∈ ℝ — space
- x⁴ ∈ [0, L_4) with x^4 ∼ x^4 + L_4 — first compact direction
- x⁵ ∈ [0, L_5) with x^5 ∼ x^5 + L_5 — second compact direction

The lengths L_4 and L_5 are the two circumferences of the
2-torus.  The torus is **flat** in this construction (no
intrinsic curvature) — what gives it nontrivial structure is
the off-diagonal metric entries with spacetime, plus the
internal off-diagonal g_45.

### A.2 — The 6×6 metric ansatz

> *Purpose: write down the form of the metric that will let
> spacetime, gauge fields, and internal shear all live together
> as components of a single tensor.*

Following the Kaluza ansatz from the primer (§7) but with two
compact directions, take

<!-- ds² = g_μν dx^μ dx^ν + g_ab (A^a_μ dx^μ + dx^a)(A^b_ν dx^ν + dx^b) -->
$$
ds^2
\;=\; g_{\mu\nu}\,dx^{\mu}\,dx^{\nu}
\;+\; g_{ab}\,(A^{a}{}_{\mu}\,dx^{\mu} + dx^{a})\,
              (A^{b}{}_{\nu}\,dx^{\nu} + dx^{b}),
$$

where the sum over a, b runs over {4, 5}.  Here:

- g_μν is the 4D spacetime metric (10 independent components).
- A^4_μ ≡ A_μ and A^5_μ ≡ B_μ are two 4D vector fields on
  spacetime — the two **gauge potentials**.
- g_ab is a constant 2×2 symmetric matrix with entries g_44,
  g_55, and g_45 = g_54 — the **internal metric** of the torus.

Expand the ansatz to read off the components of the 6×6 metric
G_AB.  Computing the cross-products:

<!-- G_μν = g_μν + g_ab A^a_μ A^b_ν -->
$$
G_{\mu\nu}
\;=\; g_{\mu\nu}
\;+\; g_{ab}\,A^{a}{}_{\mu}\,A^{b}{}_{\nu},
$$

<!-- G_μa = g_ab A^b_μ -->
$$
G_{\mu a}
\;=\; g_{ab}\,A^{b}{}_{\mu},
$$

<!-- G_ab = g_ab -->
$$
G_{ab} \;=\; g_{ab}.
$$

In block form, with Greek for spacetime and Latin for compact:

<!-- G = [ g + A·A , g·A ; A^T·g , g ] -->
$$
G_{AB}
\;=\;
\begin{pmatrix}
g_{\mu\nu} + g_{ab}A^{a}{}_{\mu}A^{b}{}_{\nu}
& g_{ab}\,A^{b}{}_{\mu} \\[4pt]
g_{ab}\,A^{b}{}_{\nu}
& g_{ab}
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

The 5D KK case is recovered by deleting the b = 5 row and
column (set g_55 = g_45 = 0 and B_μ = 0).

### A.3 — Identification: A_μ, B_μ, and the internal shear s

> *Purpose: name the pieces and connect them to physics.*

From the metric ansatz:

- **A_μ ≡ A^4_μ** is the gauge potential for U(1)_A (the
  isometry generated by ∂/∂x^4).
- **B_μ ≡ A^5_μ** is the gauge potential for U(1)_B (the
  isometry generated by ∂/∂x^5).
- The 2×2 internal block g_ab has three independent components:
  - **g_44** — diagonal scale of the first compact direction.
    Sets the natural scale of charges/momenta along x^4.
  - **g_55** — diagonal scale of the second compact direction.
    Same role for x^5.
  - **g_45 = g_54** — the **internal shear**: the off-diagonal
    coupling between the two compact directions.

A useful dimensionless parametrization of the shear:

<!-- s ≡ g_45 / √(g_44 g_55) -->
$$
s \;\equiv\; \frac{g_{45}}{\sqrt{g_{44}\,g_{55}}}.
$$

Geometric meaning of s: the two compact directions x^4 and x^5
are not orthogonal; they are tilted toward each other by an
angle θ with cos θ = s.  When s = 0 the torus is a Cartesian
product of two circles ("rectangular torus"); when s ≠ 0 it is
a sheared torus with an off-diagonal metric.

This s is the same quantity MaSt has been calling the
"shear" — the off-diagonal entry of each sheet's 2×2 internal
metric.  Identifying it concretely with g_45/√(g_44 g_55) makes
this connection explicit.

### A.4 — Cylinder condition

> *Purpose: state what is allowed to depend on what.  This is
> Klein's key simplification and it cascades through every
> subsequent step.*

All ten quantities {g_μν, A_μ, B_μ, g_44, g_55, g_45} are
functions of the four spacetime coordinates x^ρ only.  None of
them depend on x^4 or x^5.  Equivalently:

<!-- ∂_a G_BC = 0  for all B, C and for a ∈ {4, 5} -->
$$
\partial_{a}\,G_{BC} \;=\; 0
\qquad\text{for } a \in \{4, 5\}\;\text{and all } B, C.
$$

Justification: experiments to date have not resolved variations
across the compact directions (they are smaller than any probe
length we have used).  Mathematically, this is a strong
restriction that turns ∂/∂x^4 and ∂/∂x^5 into **Killing
vectors** of the metric (Section D.2 will exploit this).

For the 2×2 internal block, we additionally take the components
g_44, g_55, g_45 to be **constant in spacetime** (not varying
across x^μ either).  This is the standard KK assumption that
the size and shape of the compact dimensions are fixed once and
for all; modifying it would introduce new scalar fields
("moduli" / "dilatons") that are not the focus of this
derivation.

## Section B — Inverse metric

### B.1 — Strategy

> *Purpose: get G^AB explicitly.  We need it to compute
> Christoffel symbols.*

We want the inverse G^AB satisfying G^AB G_BC = δ^A_C.  The
ansatz from A.2 has a special block structure (Kaluza form)
that makes the inverse computable in closed form.  The trick:

- The spacetime block of the inverse is g^μν (the 4D inverse).
- The compact block of the inverse is h^ab (the 2×2 inverse of
  g_ab) plus an A·A correction.
- The mixed block of the inverse is −A^aμ (with raised index).

This is the natural generalization of the 5D KK inverse from
primer §A.2.  We verify it by explicit computation in B.3.

### B.2 — Inverse of the 2×2 internal block

> *Purpose: invert the 2×2 internal metric explicitly so we can
> use it in the rest of the calculation.*

Write g_ab as a 2×2 matrix:

<!-- g_ab = [g_44 g_45; g_45 g_55] -->
$$
g_{ab}
\;=\;
\begin{pmatrix}
g_{44} & g_{45} \\[2pt]
g_{45} & g_{55}
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Determinant:

<!-- G ≡ det(g_ab) = g_44 g_55 − g_45² -->
$$
G \;\equiv\; \det(g_{ab})
\;=\; g_{44}\,g_{55} \;-\; g_{45}^{2}.
$$

In terms of s: G = g_44 g_55 (1 − s²).  Positive definiteness
of g_ab requires g_44 > 0, g_55 > 0, and G > 0, i.e.

<!-- |s| < 1 -->
$$
|s| \;<\; 1.
$$

This is a new constraint with no 5D KK analog.  When |s| → 1
the 2×2 internal block becomes singular (the two compact
directions degenerate to a single 1D direction); we exclude
that limit.

The inverse is

<!-- h^ab = (1/G) [g_55 −g_45; −g_45 g_44] -->
$$
h^{ab}
\;=\;
\frac{1}{G}
\begin{pmatrix}
g_{55} & -g_{45} \\[2pt]
-g_{45} & g_{44}
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Note the **sign flip on the off-diagonal**: g_45 > 0 implies
h^45 < 0 and vice versa.  This sign flip will reappear when we
invert the conserved-momenta / kinetic-velocity relations in
Section E.

### B.3 — Full inverse G^AB

> *Purpose: write down G^AB in component form and verify it
> satisfies G^AB G_BC = δ^A_C.*

Claim: the inverse metric is

<!-- G^μν = g^μν -->
$$
G^{\mu\nu} \;=\; g^{\mu\nu},
$$

<!-- G^μa = -A^aμ ≡ -g^μν A^a_ν -->
$$
G^{\mu a} \;=\; -A^{a\,\mu}
\;\equiv\; -g^{\mu\nu}\,A^{a}{}_{\nu},
$$

<!-- G^ab = h^ab + A^a_μ A^bμ -->
$$
G^{ab} \;=\; h^{ab} \;+\; A^{a}{}_{\mu}\,A^{b\,\mu}.
$$

Verification: compute G^AB G_BC for each combination of A, C.

**Case 1: A = μ, C = ν.**

<!-- G^μρ G_ρν + G^μb G_bν -->
$$
G^{\mu\rho}\,G_{\rho\nu} + G^{\mu b}\,G_{b\nu}
\;=\; g^{\mu\rho}\!\left(g_{\rho\nu} + g_{ab}A^{a}{}_{\rho}A^{b}{}_{\nu}\right)
\;+\; (-A^{b\,\mu})\,g_{bc}A^{c}{}_{\nu}.
$$

The first term is δ^μ_ν + g_ab A^aμ A^b_ν (raising one index
on A^a_ρ).  The second is −g_bc A^bμ A^c_ν.  Renaming dummy
indices, these cancel cleanly:

$$
= \delta^{\mu}{}_{\nu}
\;+\; g_{ab}A^{a\,\mu}A^{b}{}_{\nu}
\;-\; g_{ab}A^{a\,\mu}A^{b}{}_{\nu}
\;=\; \delta^{\mu}{}_{\nu}. \quad\checkmark
$$

**Case 2: A = μ, C = c.**

<!-- G^μρ G_ρc + G^μb G_bc -->
$$
G^{\mu\rho}\,G_{\rho c} + G^{\mu b}\,G_{bc}
\;=\; g^{\mu\rho}\,g_{cb}\,A^{b}{}_{\rho}
\;+\; (-A^{b\,\mu})\,g_{bc}.
$$

The first is g_cb A^bμ.  The second is −g_bc A^bμ.  These are
equal in magnitude with opposite sign (using g_bc = g_cb), so

$$
= 0. \quad\checkmark
$$

**Case 3: A = a, C = ν.**

<!-- G^aρ G_ρν + G^ab G_bν -->
$$
G^{a\rho}\,G_{\rho\nu} + G^{ab}\,G_{b\nu}
\;=\; (-A^{a\,\rho})\!\left(g_{\rho\nu} + g_{cd}A^{c}{}_{\rho}A^{d}{}_{\nu}\right)
\;+\; (h^{ab} + A^{a}{}_{\sigma}A^{b\,\sigma})\,g_{bc}A^{c}{}_{\nu}.
$$

First piece: −A^a_ν − g_cd A^aρ A^c_ρ A^d_ν.  Second piece
expands to δ^a_c A^c_ν + A^a_σ A^bσ g_bc A^c_ν = A^a_ν +
g_bc A^a_σ A^bσ A^c_ν.  Combining and using h^ab g_bc = δ^a_c
in the inner contraction, the cross-terms cancel and we get 0.
Detailed bookkeeping omitted; the structure is identical to
the 5D KK verification in primer §A.2 with one extra sum over
b.

**Case 4: A = a, C = c.**

<!-- G^aρ G_ρc + G^ab G_bc -->
$$
G^{a\rho}\,G_{\rho c} + G^{ab}\,G_{bc}
\;=\; (-A^{a\,\rho})\,g_{cb}A^{b}{}_{\rho}
\;+\; (h^{ab} + A^{a}{}_{\sigma}A^{b\,\sigma})\,g_{bc}.
$$

First piece: −g_cb A^aρ A^b_ρ.  Second piece: h^ab g_bc + A^a_σ
A^bσ g_bc = δ^a_c + g_bc A^a_σ A^bσ.  The off-diagonal A·A
terms cancel:

$$
= \delta^{a}{}_{c} \;+\; g_{bc}A^{a\,\sigma}A^{b}{}_{\sigma}
                  \;-\; g_{cb}A^{a\,\rho}A^{b}{}_{\rho}
\;=\; \delta^{a}{}_{c}. \quad\checkmark
$$

All four cases check, so G^AB is correctly given by the
formulas above.

## Section C — Christoffel symbols

The general formula:

<!-- Γ^A_BC = ½ G^AD (∂_B G_DC + ∂_C G_DB − ∂_D G_BC) -->
$$
\Gamma^{A}{}_{BC}
\;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\,G^{AD}\!\left(
       \partial_{B} G_{DC}
       + \partial_{C} G_{DB}
       - \partial_{D} G_{BC}
       \right).
$$

By the cylinder condition, ∂_a (anything) = 0.  So whenever an
index in the formula is a compact index (4 or 5), the
corresponding ∂ vanishes and the term drops.

We need three families of Christoffels: pure spacetime
Γ^μ_νρ, mixed Γ^μ_aν (and equivalently Γ^a_μν), and the
fully-compact Γ^a_bc.  The Lorentz force in the 4D projection
comes entirely from Γ^μ_aν (Section E.2), so that is the
critical computation.

### C.1 — Pure spacetime Γ^μ_νρ

> *Purpose: confirm that the pure-spacetime Christoffel reduces
> to the standard 4D Levi-Civita Christoffel in the limit
> A = B = 0, with explicit corrections when the gauge fields
> are present.*

<!-- Γ^μ_νρ = ½ G^μD (∂_ν G_Dρ + ∂_ρ G_Dν − ∂_D G_νρ) -->
$$
\Gamma^{\mu}{}_{\nu\rho}
\;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\,G^{\mu D}\!\left(
       \partial_{\nu} G_{D\rho}
       + \partial_{\rho} G_{D\nu}
       - \partial_{D} G_{\nu\rho}
       \right).
$$

Split D = σ + a:

$$
\Gamma^{\mu}{}_{\nu\rho}
\;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\,g^{\mu\sigma}\!\left(\partial_{\nu} G_{\sigma\rho}
       + \partial_{\rho} G_{\sigma\nu} - \partial_{\sigma} G_{\nu\rho}\right)
\;+\; \tfrac{1}{2}\,(-A^{a\,\mu})\!\left(\partial_{\nu} G_{a\rho}
       + \partial_{\rho} G_{a\nu}\right).
$$

(The third "−∂_a G_νρ" term in the second piece vanishes by
cylinder condition.)

Substitute G_σρ = g_σρ + g_ab A^a_σ A^b_ρ and G_aρ = g_ab
A^b_ρ.  After algebra (analogous to primer §A.4), the result
is the standard 4D Levi-Civita symbol of g_μν plus
correction terms quadratic in the gauge fields:

$$
\Gamma^{\mu}{}_{\nu\rho}
\;=\; {}^{(4)}\Gamma^{\mu}{}_{\nu\rho}
\;+\; (\text{terms involving } A^a, F^a).
$$

The explicit form of the corrections is needed for full
gravitational back-reaction analysis (e.g., extracting
Maxwell's equations from the 6D Einstein equations) but is
not required for the Lorentz-force projection in Section E.
We omit it here and refer to standard KK references.  In the
A = B = 0 limit, only the first term survives, matching pure
4D GR.

### C.2 — Γ^μ_aν: where the field strengths emerge

> *Purpose: this is the key computation.  This Christoffel is
> what produces the Lorentz force in the 4D projection.*

By cylinder condition, only spacetime derivatives survive:

<!-- Γ^μ_aν = ½ G^μD (∂_a G_Dν + ∂_ν G_Da − ∂_D G_aν) -->
$$
\Gamma^{\mu}{}_{a\nu}
\;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\,G^{\mu D}\!\left(
       \partial_{a} G_{D\nu}
       + \partial_{\nu} G_{Da}
       - \partial_{D} G_{a\nu}
       \right).
$$

Label the three terms in the Christoffel formula as:
- term 1: ∂_a G_Dν — vanishes (cylinder: ∂_a = 0)
- term 2: ∂_ν G_Da — survives when D = σ (spacetime)
- term 3: −∂_D G_aν — survives when D = σ; vanishes when
  D = b (cylinder)

Now split the sum G^μD = G^μσ + G^μb.  The G^μb = −A^bμ
piece contributes nothing: in its terms, term 1 vanishes
(cylinder), term 2 gives ∂_ν G_ba which is zero (G_ba =
g_ba is constant by A.4), and term 3 gives −∂_b G_aν
which is zero (cylinder).

Only the G^μσ = g^μσ piece survives, with terms 2 and 3:

$$
\Gamma^{\mu}{}_{a\nu}
\;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\,g^{\mu\sigma}\!\left(
       \partial_{\nu} G_{\sigma a}
       - \partial_{\sigma} G_{a\nu}
       \right).
$$

Substitute G^μσ = g^μσ, G_σa = g_ab A^b_σ, G_aν = g_ab A^b_ν:

$$
\Gamma^{\mu}{}_{a\nu}
\;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\,g^{\mu\sigma}\,g_{ab}\!\left(
       \partial_{\nu} A^{b}{}_{\sigma}
       - \partial_{\sigma} A^{b}{}_{\nu}
       \right).
$$

Recognize the antisymmetric combination as the **field
strength** of the b-th gauge potential:

<!-- F^b_νσ ≡ ∂_ν A^b_σ − ∂_σ A^b_ν -->
$$
F^{b}{}_{\nu\sigma}
\;\equiv\; \partial_{\nu} A^{b}{}_{\sigma}
       \;-\; \partial_{\sigma} A^{b}{}_{\nu}.
$$

(Antisymmetric in νσ.)  Then:

$$
\boxed{\;
\Gamma^{\mu}{}_{a\nu}
\;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\,g_{ab}\,F^{b\,\mu}{}_{\nu}
\;}
$$

where F^bμ_ν ≡ g^μσ F^b_νσ.  Two specific cases:

<!-- Γ^μ_4ν = ½ (g_44 F^Aμ_ν + g_45 F^Bμ_ν) -->
$$
\Gamma^{\mu}{}_{4\nu}
\;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\!\left(g_{44}\,F^{A\,\mu}{}_{\nu}
       + g_{45}\,F^{B\,\mu}{}_{\nu}\right),
$$

<!-- Γ^μ_5ν = ½ (g_45 F^Aμ_ν + g_55 F^Bμ_ν) -->
$$
\Gamma^{\mu}{}_{5\nu}
\;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\!\left(g_{45}\,F^{A\,\mu}{}_{\nu}
       + g_{55}\,F^{B\,\mu}{}_{\nu}\right).
$$

**Key observation.**  When g_45 = 0 (no shear), Γ^μ_4ν depends
only on F^A and Γ^μ_5ν only on F^B — the two U(1) gauge
fields decouple completely.  When g_45 ≠ 0, the shear cross-
couples them: motion in x^4 picks up a Lorentz-like force from
F^B in proportion to g_45, and vice versa.

This is the geometric mechanism by which the internal shear
mixes the two charges — and the first concrete prediction of
the 2-torus generalization that has no analog in 5D KK.

### C.3 — Γ^a_μν: the back-reaction on the compact directions

> *Purpose: completeness; we don't need the explicit form for
> the projection but we note its presence.*

By analogous algebra (with the dual index in the upper position),

<!-- Γ^a_μν = ½ h^ab g_bc (other sign combos) -->
$$
\Gamma^{a}{}_{\mu\nu}
\;=\; -\tfrac{1}{2}\!\left(\partial_{\mu} A^{a}{}_{\nu}
       + \partial_{\nu} A^{a}{}_{\mu}\right)
   \;+\; (\text{terms involving 4D Christoffels and A·A products}).
$$

This Christoffel governs how compact velocity is generated by
spacetime motion.  In the 4D projection, it shows up in the
"compact-component geodesic" — but that equation is
automatically satisfied because P_a is conserved (Section
D.2).  So we don't need its explicit form for the spacetime
geodesic projection.

### C.4 — Fully-compact Γ^a_bc and mixed Γ^μ_ab

> *Purpose: confirm these vanish (or are inessential).*

Γ^a_bc = ½ G^aD (∂_b G_Dc + ∂_c G_Db − ∂_D G_bc).  All three
derivatives have a compact index, so all vanish by cylinder.

<!-- Γ^a_bc = 0 -->
$$
\Gamma^{a}{}_{bc} \;=\; 0.
$$

Similarly Γ^μ_ab: ∂_a, ∂_b vanish, but ∂_μ G_ab = 0 because
g_ab is constant in spacetime (A.4).  So

<!-- Γ^μ_ab = 0 -->
$$
\Gamma^{\mu}{}_{ab} \;=\; 0.
$$

These vanish identically.  In the 4D geodesic projection
(Section E.1), the term Γ^μ_ab ẋ^a ẋ^b therefore vanishes —
a useful simplification.

## Section D — 6D geodesic equation and conserved compact momenta

### D.1 — The 6D geodesic

> *Purpose: write down the equation of motion for the test
> particle in 6D.  Subsequent sections decompose it.*

A massive test particle of rest mass m follows a timelike
geodesic in 6D:

<!-- d²x^A/dτ² + Γ^A_BC ẋ^B ẋ^C = 0,  G_AB ẋ^A ẋ^B = -c² -->
$$
\frac{d^{2}x^{A}}{d\tau^{2}}
\;+\; \Gamma^{A}{}_{BC}\,\dot{x}^{B}\,\dot{x}^{C} \;=\; 0,
\qquad
G_{AB}\,\dot{x}^{A}\,\dot{x}^{B} \;=\; -c^{2},
$$

where ẋ^A = dx^A/dτ.  The proper-time normalization is
preserved by the geodesic equation.

Two of these six equations are for the compact components
(A = 4, 5), and four are for the spacetime components (A = μ).
We treat them differently: the compact equations have
**conservation laws** that solve them implicitly (Section D.2);
the spacetime equations are what we project to recover the
4D physics (Section E).

### D.2 — Killing directions x^4 and x^5 → conserved compact momenta

> *Purpose: turn cylinder condition into conservation laws.
> This is the deepest step in the whole derivation.*

Under the cylinder condition, the metric does not depend on x^4
or x^5.  Equivalently, the vector fields

<!-- ξ_(4)^A = (0, 0, 0, 0, 1, 0) ,  ξ_(5)^A = (0, 0, 0, 0, 0, 1) -->
$$
\xi_{(4)}^{A} \;=\; \delta^{A}{}_{4}, \qquad
\xi_{(5)}^{A} \;=\; \delta^{A}{}_{5}
$$

are **Killing vectors** of the metric: the Lie derivative
L_ξ G_AB vanishes for both.  By Killing's theorem, for any
Killing vector ξ, the inner product ξ_A ẋ^A is conserved along
any geodesic:

<!-- d(ξ_A ẋ^A)/dτ = 0 -->
$$
\frac{d}{d\tau}\!\left(\xi_{A}\,\dot{x}^{A}\right) \;=\; 0.
$$

For our two Killing vectors:

<!-- P_a ≡ ξ_(a)^A G_AB ẋ^B = G_aB ẋ^B,  conserved -->
$$
P_{a}
\;\equiv\; G_{aB}\,\dot{x}^{B}
\;=\; G_{a\nu}\,\dot{x}^{\nu} \;+\; G_{ab}\,\dot{x}^{b},
$$

<!-- d P_a / dτ = 0 -->
$$
\frac{dP_{a}}{d\tau} \;=\; 0.
$$

Substituting G_aν = g_ab A^b_ν and G_ab = g_ab:

<!-- P_a = g_ab (A^b_ν ẋ^ν + ẋ^b) -->
$$
P_{a} \;=\; g_{ab}\!\left(A^{b}{}_{\nu}\,\dot{x}^{\nu}
                          + \dot{x}^{b}\right).
$$

Define the **gauge-covariant compact velocity**:

<!-- w^b ≡ A^b_ν ẋ^ν + ẋ^b -->
$$
w^{b} \;\equiv\; A^{b}{}_{\nu}\,\dot{x}^{\nu} \;+\; \dot{x}^{b}.
$$

Then

<!-- P_a = g_ab w^b -->
$$
\boxed{\;P_{a} \;=\; g_{ab}\,w^{b}\;}
$$

is conserved.  Two conservation laws (a = 4 and a = 5), giving
two independent constants of motion.

The relation between conserved P_a and kinetic w^b is **not
diagonal** when g_45 ≠ 0:

<!-- P_4 = g_44 w^4 + g_45 w^5 -->
$$
P_{4} \;=\; g_{44}\,w^{4} \;+\; g_{45}\,w^{5},
$$

<!-- P_5 = g_45 w^4 + g_55 w^5 -->
$$
P_{5} \;=\; g_{45}\,w^{4} \;+\; g_{55}\,w^{5}.
$$

Inverting using h^ab from B.2:

<!-- w^4 = (g_55 P_4 - g_45 P_5)/G -->
$$
w^{4} \;=\; \frac{g_{55}\,P_{4} \;-\; g_{45}\,P_{5}}{G},
$$

<!-- w^5 = (-g_45 P_4 + g_44 P_5)/G -->
$$
w^{5} \;=\; \frac{-g_{45}\,P_{4} \;+\; g_{44}\,P_{5}}{G}.
$$

This is the seed of the "shear mixing" effect that surfaces in
Section E.

### D.3 — Identification with electric charge

> *Purpose: connect the conserved P_a to the empirical notion
> of charge.*

Following the primer §10's derivation for 5D KK: in the 5D case
the conserved P_4 is identified with the particle's electric
charge Q (up to an overall constant set by units).  The
correspondence is

<!-- Q = m P_4 / (g_44)   [5D KK, schematic] -->
$$
Q \;\propto\; m\,\frac{P_{4}}{g_{44}} \quad (\text{5D KK, schematic}).
$$

The exact prefactor depends on conventions for the periodicity
of x^4 and the units chosen for the gauge potential.  The
operationally useful statement is: **conserved compact
momentum = quantized electric charge**, with the quantization
arising from the periodicity of the compact coordinate
(see Section D.4 below for the quantization argument).

For the 2-torus, we have *two* conserved compact momenta P_4
and P_5.  By the same logic, there are **two electric charges**
— one per U(1):

<!-- Q_A ∝ P_4,  Q_B ∝ P_5 -->
$$
Q_{A} \;\propto\; P_{4}, \qquad Q_{B} \;\propto\; P_{5}.
$$

A particle that has nonzero P_4 but zero P_5 is "charged under
U(1)_A but not U(1)_B."  A particle with both nonzero is
charged under both.  Particles with both zero are
electromagnetically neutral with respect to either U(1).

We will use Q_A and Q_B interchangeably with P_4 and P_5 for the
rest of this derivation; the proportionality constants are
fixed by units and don't affect the structural arguments.

### D.4 — Charge quantization (brief)

> *Purpose: state the quantization condition that follows from
> compact periodicity.*

The compact coordinates x^4 and x^5 are periodic with periods
L_4 and L_5.  A wavefunction on the torus must be single-
valued: ψ(x^4 + L_4) = ψ(x^4) and similarly for x^5.  This
requires the compact momenta to be integer multiples of h/L_a:

<!-- P_a = n_a h / L_a ,  n_a ∈ ℤ -->
$$
P_{a} \;=\; \frac{n_{a}\,h}{L_{a}},
\qquad n_{a} \in \mathbb{Z}.
$$

Therefore Q_A and Q_B are quantized integers (or rationals
with a fixed denominator set by the unit conventions).  This
is the standard KK derivation of charge quantization — see
primer §10.

## Section E — Projection to 4D

### E.1 — Spacetime components of the geodesic

> *Purpose: write out the 6D geodesic for the spacetime
> components, term by term.*

Take A = μ in the 6D geodesic.  Expanding Γ^μ_BC ẋ^B ẋ^C with
B, C running over all 6:

<!-- d²x^μ/dτ² + Γ^μ_νρ ẋ^ν ẋ^ρ + 2 Γ^μ_aν ẋ^a ẋ^ν + Γ^μ_ab ẋ^a ẋ^b = 0 -->
$$
\frac{d^{2}x^{\mu}}{d\tau^{2}}
\;+\; \Gamma^{\mu}{}_{\nu\rho}\,\dot{x}^{\nu}\,\dot{x}^{\rho}
\;+\; 2\,\Gamma^{\mu}{}_{a\nu}\,\dot{x}^{a}\,\dot{x}^{\nu}
\;+\; \Gamma^{\mu}{}_{ab}\,\dot{x}^{a}\,\dot{x}^{b}
\;=\; 0.
$$

The factor of 2 in the third term is because the cross-term
Γ^μ_aν ẋ^a ẋ^ν appears twice when summing (once as Γ^μ_aν,
once as Γ^μ_νa, equal by symmetry of the lower indices).

By C.4, the fourth term vanishes (Γ^μ_ab = 0).  So the
spacetime geodesic reduces to

<!-- d²x^μ/dτ² + Γ^μ_νρ ẋ^ν ẋ^ρ + 2 Γ^μ_aν ẋ^a ẋ^ν = 0 -->
$$
\frac{d^{2}x^{\mu}}{d\tau^{2}}
\;+\; \Gamma^{\mu}{}_{\nu\rho}\,\dot{x}^{\nu}\,\dot{x}^{\rho}
\;+\; 2\,\Gamma^{\mu}{}_{a\nu}\,\dot{x}^{a}\,\dot{x}^{\nu}
\;=\; 0.
$$

The first two terms are the standard 4D geodesic with Γ^μ_νρ
(which includes A·A back-reaction corrections from C.1).  The
third term — quadratic in velocity, mixing spacetime and
compact components — is the Lorentz force.

### E.2 — Two-charge Lorentz force

> *Purpose: substitute Γ^μ_aν from C.2 into the third term and
> rearrange to look like a Lorentz force law.*

Substituting Γ^μ_aν = ½ g_ab F^bμ_ν from C.2:

<!-- 2 Γ^μ_aν ẋ^a ẋ^ν = g_ab F^bμ_ν ẋ^a ẋ^ν -->
$$
2\,\Gamma^{\mu}{}_{a\nu}\,\dot{x}^{a}\,\dot{x}^{\nu}
\;=\; g_{ab}\,F^{b\,\mu}{}_{\nu}\,\dot{x}^{a}\,\dot{x}^{\nu}.
$$

Rearranging D.2's definition w^a = A^a_ν ẋ^ν + ẋ^a gives
ẋ^a = w^a − A^a_ρ ẋ^ρ.  Substituting:

$$
g_{ab}\,F^{b\,\mu}{}_{\nu}\,\dot{x}^{a}\,\dot{x}^{\nu}
\;=\; g_{ab}\,w^{a}\,F^{b\,\mu}{}_{\nu}\,\dot{x}^{\nu}
\;-\; g_{ab}\,A^{a}{}_{\rho}\,\dot{x}^{\rho}\,F^{b\,\mu}{}_{\nu}\,\dot{x}^{\nu}.
$$

Use g_ab w^a = P_b (D.2) on the first term:

$$
=\; P_{b}\,F^{b\,\mu}{}_{\nu}\,\dot{x}^{\nu}
\;-\; g_{ab}\,A^{a}{}_{\rho}\,F^{b\,\mu}{}_{\nu}\,\dot{x}^{\rho}\,\dot{x}^{\nu}.
$$

The first piece is the **two-charge Lorentz force**:

<!-- P_4 F^Aμ_ν ẋ^ν + P_5 F^Bμ_ν ẋ^ν = Q_A F^Aμ_ν ẋ^ν + Q_B F^Bμ_ν ẋ^ν -->
$$
\boxed{\;
P_{4}\,F^{A\,\mu}{}_{\nu}\,\dot{x}^{\nu}
\;+\; P_{5}\,F^{B\,\mu}{}_{\nu}\,\dot{x}^{\nu}
\;=\; Q_{A}\,F^{A\,\mu}{}_{\nu}\,\dot{x}^{\nu}
   \;+\; Q_{B}\,F^{B\,\mu}{}_{\nu}\,\dot{x}^{\nu}
\;}
$$

A particle with charges (Q_A, Q_B) feels two independent
Lorentz forces — one from each U(1) field strength.  When
Q_B = 0 the particle is invisible to F^B (and conversely).
This is the textbook KK result, doubled.

The second piece (g_ab A^a_ρ F^bμ_ν ẋ^ρ ẋ^ν) is a quadratic-
in-velocity correction whose role in the 4D theory is to
combine with the spacetime Christoffel terms to produce the
Maxwell stress-energy tensor in the 4D Einstein equations.
It is present in 5D KK as well and is not new physics for the
2-torus generalization; we don't expand on it here.

### E.3 — Shear-induced mixing of the two charges

> *Purpose: identify the kinematic effect of the shear g_45.*

The shear g_45 has already produced two effects in this
derivation:

1. **In the Christoffel symbols (C.2):** Γ^μ_4ν includes a
   g_45-weighted F^B contribution, and Γ^μ_5ν a g_45-weighted
   F^A contribution.  These cross-couple the two field
   strengths in the equations of motion.
2. **In the relation P_a ↔ w^b (D.2):** when g_45 ≠ 0, P_4
   and P_5 are not independently equal to g_44 w^4 and g_55
   w^5; they are linear combinations of both kinetic
   velocities.

Effect (2) has a striking consequence.  Suppose a particle has
P_4 = Q_A ≠ 0 and P_5 = Q_B = 0 — a "pure-Q_A eigenstate."
From the inversion in D.2:

<!-- w^4 = g_55 Q_A / G ,  w^5 = -g_45 Q_A / G -->
$$
w^{4} \;=\; \frac{g_{55}}{G}\,Q_{A},
\qquad
w^{5} \;=\; -\frac{g_{45}}{G}\,Q_{A}.
$$

When g_45 ≠ 0, w^5 ≠ 0 — the particle has nonzero gauge-
covariant velocity in the *second* compact direction, even
though its second charge Q_B = P_5 vanishes.  This is the
**kinematic shear mixing**: pure-charge eigenstates of one
U(1) are not at rest in the orthogonal compact direction.

In MaSt language: a particle that is a pure tube-winding
eigenstate (n_4 = 1, n_5 = 0) still has nonzero ring-direction
gauge-covariant momentum in proportion to the shear.  This is
the origin of the (n_r − s n_t) combination in the MaSt mass
formula.

The full quantitative connection — recovering MaSt's
μ² = (n_t/ε)² + (n_r − s n_t)² from the 6D mass-shell
condition with shear — requires the photon-on-torus mass
formula and is treated separately.  This derivation only
establishes the mechanism: the shear lives in the inverse of
the 2×2 internal block, and it shows up whenever you convert
between conserved P_a and kinetic w^b.

### E.4 — Summary of the projected geodesic

Combining E.1, E.2, E.3, the 4D-projected geodesic of a massive
test particle is

<!-- d²x^μ/dτ² + Γ^μ_νρ ẋ^ν ẋ^ρ = -(Q_A F^Aμ_ν + Q_B F^Bμ_ν) ẋ^ν + corrections -->
$$
\frac{d^{2}x^{\mu}}{d\tau^{2}}
\;+\; \Gamma^{\mu}{}_{\nu\rho}\,\dot{x}^{\nu}\,\dot{x}^{\rho}
\;=\; -\!\left(Q_{A}\,F^{A\,\mu}{}_{\nu}
              + Q_{B}\,F^{B\,\mu}{}_{\nu}\right)\!\dot{x}^{\nu}
   \;+\; (\text{quadratic-A corrections}).
$$

This is **two-charge electrodynamics** in 4D, derived from a
6D massive geodesic with no input other than the metric ansatz
and the cylinder condition.  In the limit g_45 → 0, the two
U(1)s decouple and the system becomes literally two
independent Maxwell sectors.  Turning on the shear g_45
introduces the cross-coupling that mixes them in the
Christoffel symbols and in the conserved-charge / kinetic-
velocity relation.

## Section F — Catalog: what generalizes vs what is new

### F.1 — What generalizes cleanly from 5D KK

Each of these reproduces the 5D KK case when the second compact
direction is removed (delete the b = 5 row and column of the
6×6 metric — equivalently, take the manifold to be M⁴ × S¹
instead of M⁴ × T², which automatically zeroes B_μ, g_45, and
the entire G_μ5 / G_55 / G_5ν block).  Note: smoothly taking
g_55 → 0 with the 6×6 structure intact would make the internal
2×2 block singular; the 5D limit is a structural reduction, not
a parameter limit.

- **Metric ansatz** (A.2): the Kaluza form ds² = g_μν dx^μ dx^ν +
  g_ab (A^a + dx^a)(A^b + dx^b) extends term-by-term from one to
  two compact directions.
- **Inverse metric** (B.3): the formula G^μν = g^μν, G^μa =
  −A^aμ, G^ab = h^ab + A·A holds with h^ab now a 2×2 inverse.
- **Cylinder condition** (A.4): straightforwardly extends to
  ∂_a vanishing for both compact indices.
- **Christoffel structure** (C.2): the formula Γ^μ_aν = ½ g_ab
  F^bμ_ν is the same as 5D KK with the "1" replaced by "a, b
  ∈ {4, 5}."
- **Conservation of compact momenta** (D.2): two Killing vectors
  give two conserved P_a, just as one gives one.
- **Two-charge Lorentz force** (E.2): one Lorentz force per
  U(1); generalizes additively.
- **Charge quantization** (D.4): each compact direction
  quantizes independently.

### F.2 — What is structurally new on the 2-torus

These have no 5D KK analog:

- **U(1)×U(1) gauge group** instead of U(1).  Two independent
  gauge potentials A_μ and B_μ with their own field strengths
  F^A and F^B.
- **Internal shear g_45.**  A new metric component, off-
  diagonal within the 2×2 internal block.  No counterpart in
  5D KK (where the internal block is 1×1).
- **Christoffel cross-coupling** (C.2).  When g_45 ≠ 0,
  Γ^μ_4ν depends on F^B and Γ^μ_5ν depends on F^A — the
  shear cross-couples the two gauge sectors at the
  Christoffel level.
- **Kinematic shear mixing** (E.3).  Pure-Q_A eigenstates have
  nonzero w^5 in proportion to g_45.  Pure-Q_B eigenstates
  have nonzero w^4 likewise.  This is the geometric mechanism
  underlying the (n_r − s n_t) combination in MaSt's mass
  formula.
- **Positivity constraint |s| < 1.**  Required by positive-
  definiteness of the 2×2 internal block.  When |s| → 1 the
  internal metric becomes singular — the two compact
  directions degenerate.  Restricts the allowed shear range,
  with no analog in 5D KK.

## Lemma (Track 2 result)

We have shown:

> **(F4) U(1)×U(1) gauge structure.**  Compactifying on a
> 2-torus produces two independent U(1) gauge potentials A_μ
> and B_μ from the off-diagonal metric entries G_μ4 and G_μ5.
> The cylinder condition gives two conserved compact momenta
> P_4 and P_5 (Killing vectors ∂/∂x^4 and ∂/∂x^5), identified
> as charges Q_A and Q_B of a particle.
>
> **(F5) Two-charge Lorentz force.**  The 4D projection of the
> 6D massive geodesic includes the Lorentz-force term
> Q_A F^Aμ_ν ẋ^ν + Q_B F^Bμ_ν ẋ^ν.  In the limit of no shear
> (g_45 = 0) the two U(1) sectors are exactly decoupled.
>
> **(F6) Internal shear.**  The 2×2 internal block g_ab has
> one off-diagonal entry g_45 (parametrized by s = g_45 /
> √(g_44 g_55), with |s| < 1) that has no analog in 5D KK.
> Its presence has two effects:
>
> (i) **Christoffel cross-coupling** — Γ^μ_4ν acquires a
>     g_45 F^Bμ_ν term and Γ^μ_5ν a g_45 F^Aμ_ν term,
>     mixing the two field strengths in the equations of
>     motion.
>
> (ii) **Kinematic mixing** — the relation between conserved
>      P_a and kinetic w^b is non-diagonal: P_a = g_ab w^b
>      with g_ab non-diagonal.  Pure-Q_A eigenstates have
>      nonzero w^5 = −g_45 Q_A / G; pure-Q_B eigenstates
>      have nonzero w^4 = −g_45 Q_B / G.

F4–F6 provide the geometric and dynamical framework — 6D
metric structure, conserved compact momenta, two-charge
Lorentz force, and kinematic shear-mixing mechanism — used by
the subsequent derivations of Program 1.
